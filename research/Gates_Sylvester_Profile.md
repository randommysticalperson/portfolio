## Biography

Sylvester James Gates Jr. is one of the most distinguished theoretical physicists in the United States. He holds the **Clark Leadership Chair in Science** at the University of Maryland, where he has also been named a **Distinguished University Professor**, a **University System of Maryland Regents Professor**, and formerly the **John S. Toll Professor of Physics**. He additionally served as an affiliate professor of mathematics.

Gates earned **two Bachelor of Science degrees** — in physics and in mathematics — as well as his **Ph.D. in physics**, all from the **Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)**. In **1984**, he co-authored *Superspace: One Thousand and One Lessons in Supersymmetry*, the first comprehensive textbook on supersymmetry, and joined the UMD faculty as an associate professor. Four years later, in **1988**, he became the **first African American to hold an endowed chair in physics at a major U.S. research university** — a landmark achievement in the history of American science.

## Research

Gates is internationally recognized for his **pioneering work in supersymmetry and supergravity**, fields intimately connected to string theory and the mathematical foundations of fundamental physics. A central focus of his recent research involves **Adinkras** — graph-theoretic and algebraic structures used to represent supersymmetric algebras — which he has explored through the lens of Coxeter group representations and dimensional extensions of supersymmetry.

## Public Service & Leadership

Gates has been a prominent voice in science policy and education advocacy. He served on the **U.S. President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST)** under President Obama, the **National Commission on Forensic Science**, and the **Maryland State Board of Education**. He has served as president of both the **National Society of Black Physicists** and the **American Physical Society** — the premier professional organization for physicists in the United States.

---

## Public Outreach

Gates is a prolific science communicator, having appeared in dozens of video documentaries. Notable public engagements include a *Q2C Festival 2009* talk titled *"Does Reality Have a Genetic Basis?"*, a *C-SPAN Q&A Interview*, and an [On Being interview](https://onbeing.org/programs/s-james-gates-uncovering-the-codes-for-reality/) on uncovering the codes for reality. He has also written non-scientific essays on the universality of creativity across the liberal arts and sciences.
