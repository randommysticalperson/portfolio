## Biography

Sylvester James Gates Jr. is one of the most distinguished theoretical physicists in the United States. He holds the **Clark Leadership Chair in Science** at the University of Maryland, where he has also been named a **Distinguished University Professor**, a **University System of Maryland Regents Professor**, and formerly the **John S. Toll Professor of Physics**. He additionally served as an affiliate professor of mathematics.

Gates earned **two Bachelor of Science degrees** — in physics and in mathematics — as well as his **Ph.D. in physics**, all from the **Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)**. In **1984**, he co-authored *Superspace: One Thousand and One Lessons in Supersymmetry*, the first comprehensive textbook on supersymmetry, and joined the UMD faculty as an associate professor. Four years later, in **1988**, he became the **first African American to hold an endowed chair in physics at a major U.S. research university** — a landmark achievement in the history of American science.

## Research

Gates is internationally recognized for his **pioneering work in supersymmetry and supergravity**, fields intimately connected to string theory and the mathematical foundations of fundamental physics. His research programme spans several decades and touches on some of the deepest open questions in theoretical physics, including the nature of spacetime at the Planck scale, the algebraic structure of supersymmetric theories, and the relationship between physics and information theory.

### Adinkras

A central and distinctive focus of Gates's recent research involves **Adinkras** — combinatorial and graph-theoretic structures that encode the transformation rules of supersymmetric multiplets. Named after the visual symbols used in West African Adinkra cloth, these mathematical objects provide a powerful diagrammatic language for classifying and comparing supersymmetric representations. Gates has investigated their equivalence classes via Coxeter group representations and explored their dimensional extension from worldlines to worldsheets.

### Supersymmetry and Supergravity

Gates's foundational contributions to **supersymmetry** — the theoretical framework proposing a symmetry between bosons and fermions — and **supergravity** — its local, gravity-incorporating extension — have shaped the field for over four decades. His 1984 textbook *Superspace: One Thousand and One Lessons in Supersymmetry* (co-authored with M. T. Grisaru, M. Roček, and W. Siegel) remains a standard reference in the field.

## Public Service & Leadership

Gates has been a prominent voice in science policy and education advocacy at the highest levels of government and academia. He served on the **U.S. President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (PCAST)** under President Obama, the **National Commission on Forensic Science**, and the **Maryland State Board of Education**. He has served as president of both the **National Society of Black Physicists** and the **American Physical Society** — the premier professional organization for physicists in the United States.

His policy contributions include co-authoring the *Prepare & Inspire* report, a landmark PCAST document on science and mathematics education reform in the United States.

## Public Outreach & Science Communication

Gates is a prolific science communicator, having appeared in dozens of video documentaries — including five in 2015 alone. Notable public engagements include:

- *Q2C Festival 2009* talk: *"Does Reality Have a Genetic Basis?"*
- *C-SPAN Q&A Interview* on physics and education
- [On Being interview](https://onbeing.org/programs/s-james-gates-uncovering-the-codes-for-reality/): *"Uncovering the Codes for Reality"*
- *The Secret Life of Jim Gates* — documentary feature
- Talks on the Higgs Boson and supersymmetry for general audiences

He has also written non-scientific essays exploring the universality of creativity across the liberal arts and sciences, and on the relationship between science, irrationality, and innovation.
